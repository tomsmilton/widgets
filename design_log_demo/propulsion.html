PROPULSION SYSTEM OPTIMIZATION
REF: ENG-2025-03-01 | LEAD: J. ROBERTS

Overview

This log documents our experimental approach to optimizing the propulsion system for the A7 prototype. Key focus areas include thermal efficiency, power-to-weight ratio improvements, and reliability under extreme conditions.

PERFORMANCE METRICS
> Thermal efficiency:      37.2% → 42.8% (↑ 15.1%)
> Power-to-weight ratio:   1.35 kW/kg → 1.72 kW/kg
> Operating temperature:   -40°C to +85°C
> MTBF:                    16,500 hours
> Avg. power consumption:  22.7% reduction

Initial tests indicate that our modified cooling system design has successfully addressed the thermal runaway issues encountered in previous iterations. Recommendations for further improvements have been documented in the technical specifications section.

Technical Approach

Our optimization strategy involved a multi-phase approach:

  1. Computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulation to identify inefficiencies in the original design
  2. Material substitution for critical components to improve thermal characteristics
  3. Redesigned geometry to optimize flow dynamics and reduce turbulence
  4. Implementation of advanced manufacturing techniques to maintain precise tolerances

The most significant improvements came from redesigning the combustion chamber geometry, which allowed for more complete fuel utilization and reduced hotspots that were causing premature component degradation.

Component Analysis

Component Original Material Optimized Material Performance Change
Combustion Chamber Inconel 718 CM-7 Composite +18.5% thermal efficiency
Fuel Injectors Titanium Alloy Titanium-Vanadium +12.3% flow uniformity
Nozzle Assembly Niobium Alloy Carbon-Carbon Composite -32.7% weight
Control Valves Stainless Steel Titanium-Niobium +27.8% response time

The material substitutions provided significant improvements in system performance while also reducing overall weight. The CM-7 composite material used in the combustion chamber was particularly effective at maintaining structural integrity under extreme thermal conditions.

Future Developments

Based on these promising results, we've identified several areas for further optimization:

  • Integration of ceramic matrix composite materials for critical high-temperature components
  • Implementation of adaptive control algorithms to optimize performance across the operational envelope
  • Extended durability testing to validate the projected MTBF improvements

The next phase of development will focus on improving reliability in extreme environmental conditions, with particular emphasis on performance in high-humidity and salt-spray environments.
